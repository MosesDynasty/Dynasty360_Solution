How to Import Cars to Nigeria Without Being Scammed

By the Dynasty360 Automotive Team | Updated July 2025

Importing a car into Nigeria can be both rewarding and risky. Many people lose millions due to scams, fake agents, and unexpected duties. Here’s how to do it the smart way:

1. Choose a Trusted Car Dropshipper

Never deal with individuals without verified history. Use companies like Dynasty360 with references, website, and verifiable clients.

2. Know the Cost Breakdown

  • ✅ Car price (auction or dealership)
  • ✅ Shipping fee
  • ✅ Clearing & duty
  • ✅ Delivery to your location

3. Avoid Red Flags

If someone offers to clear your car for half the price — it’s likely a scam. Always demand receipts and physical documentation.

4. Use Verified Port Routes

We recommend clearing through Lagos (Tincan or Apapa), or Port Harcourt — and using licensed clearing agents.
